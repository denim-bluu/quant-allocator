## 3. Selection

**Rule applied:** flagships = stack of 4 (all four axes ≥4) **and**
feasibility ≥4. Exactly two cards qualify. Quick wins = time-to-first-demo
≤2 weeks with distinct decision coverage.

### Flagships

1. **X1 · Tier & Power Atlas.** The campaign thesis made measurable, uniquely
   enabled by our simulator (all three tiers of the same known truth — built
   for exactly this), unpublished anywhere (Sweeps B and C), and structurally
   first: it produces the PowerGate registry every other card consumes. The
   senior-role story writes itself: "here is the platform analytic, here is
   what survives at each transparency tier, here is the error you eat."
2. **S1 · Hierarchical Bayesian alpha engine.** Sweep C's method of choice at
   36–60 observations, the strongest current-seat card (posterior skill →
   select and size, today), genuine white space per Sweep D (no vendor
   productizes it), and the foundation for the phase-2 cards (P1, P2).

### Quick wins

1. **X2 · Transparency playground** — the public-portfolio centerpiece and
   the thesis's communication device; ships v1 on a starter grid, upgrades
   when atlas vol. 1 lands.
2. **S2 · Uncertainty-honest tear-sheet engine** (returns-only rung) — the
   always-on layer for every manager; first consumer of the Interval design
   system; immediate current-seat value.
3. **E1 · Trust-preserving transparency ladder** (memo) — zero-code, usable
   in engagement conversations immediately, covers ENGAGE which the build
   slate otherwise leaves thin, and protects the data tier everything else
   depends on.

### Runners-up — deferred on stated grounds, not on value

- **P2 (fusion X-ray)** ties X1 for the highest axis total in the portfolio
  and is the ceiling of the whole program. Deferred on feasibility (2/5): it
  is a research program whose calibration inputs are literally X1's outputs
  and whose skill posteriors are S1's. It is the **designated phase-2
  flagship candidate** — the flagships were chosen partly *because* they
  de-risk it.
- **P1 (allocation)** stacks four axes but strictly depends on S1; it is the
  first flagship-scale extension after the 90 days.
- **S3/S4 (lab, sell-discipline)** carry the strongest senior-role scores
  (5s) but seat-3: they serve the transparent few, not the roster. They lead
  the backlog for the senior-role axis, and X1 vol. 2 computes their gates.
- **M3 (drawdown alarms)** just missed quick-win on decision-coverage
  tiebreak (E1 covers ENGAGE, otherwise absent); it is the first backlog
  item and slots into S2's pack when built.

## 4. What we deliberately do not build

**Do-not-build (Sweep C noise verdicts at 36–60 obs; revisit only if the
evaluable universe reaches hundreds):** standalone persistence rankings ·
FDR luck-screens at roster scale · regime-split alpha tables ·
conditional-beta (time-varying) alphas · returns-based style-drift
*inference* (measurement at E/P instead).

**Buy, don't build (Sweep D):** security-level factor risk models
(Barra/Axioma) · portfolio aggregation/look-through plumbing (Novus-class;
thin-build only if workflow demands) · proprietary crowding feeds (MSCI;
we build the free 13F sanity-check rung only) · commodity performance math
(adopt `empyrical-reloaded`) · optimization solvers (build on
skfolio/Riskfolio backends).

**Standing non-goals (spec §4)** remain in force: no security-level alpha
research, no execution/TCA, nothing intraday, no derivative-pricing engines,
no VaR-plumbing rebuilds, no chatbots without a decision hook.

compound.
