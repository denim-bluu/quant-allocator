# Sweep C — Methods Robustness at 36–60 Monthly Observations

## 1. State of practice

The standard toolkit for manager evaluation was built on mutual-fund panels with hundreds of funds and 20+ years of data. An allocator with 36–60 monthly observations per manager and tens of managers operates in a different statistical regime, and the honest starting point is the arithmetic of the t-statistic: for a factor alpha, t ≈ IR_ann × √(T/12). A genuinely good manager with a true annualized information ratio of 0.5 produces an expected t of ~1.0 at T = 60 — power below ~30% against a 5% two-sided test. Equivalently, the Sharpe-ratio standard error (Lo 2002) is ≈ √((1 + SR²/2)/T); at T = 48 the 95% CI on an annualized Sharpe of 1.0 is roughly [0, 2] before correcting for the positive serial correlation that illiquid strategies exhibit (Getmansky–Lo–Makarov 2004), which widens it further. Time-series inference on a single manager at this N is therefore structurally weak; power must come from elsewhere — the cross-section of managers (hierarchical shrinkage) or the cross-section of positions and trades (holdings-based metrics, where effective N is trades × breadth, not months). That reframing drives every verdict below.

Verdict key: **Robust** = usable for decisions as designed; **Shrink** = usable-with-shrinkage/priors/simulation calibration, never as a raw significance test; **Noise** = noise-theater at this N. Tiers: T1 = monthly returns, T2 = exposure summaries, T3 = position/trade level.

| Method | What it estimates | Min tier | Power at 36–60 monthly obs (reasoning) | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| OLS factor alpha | Skill after static betas | T1 | t ≈ IR√(T/12); IR 0.5 → t≈1.0–1.1; power <30%. Beta estimation error adds alpha variance (Pástor–Stambaugh) | Shrink |
| Conditional (time-varying beta) alpha | Skill with state-dependent betas | T1 | Doubles+ parameter count on 36–60 obs; df exhaustion; overfits | Noise |
| Sharpe RBSA | Style/asset-class mix | T1 | Constrained weights are stabilized by the non-negativity/sum constraints; adequate for *exposure description*, not alpha | Shrink (descriptive) |
| Fung–Hsieh 7-factor | Macro/trend exposures + alpha | T1 | 7 regressors on 36 obs = 28 df; option-straddle factors are noisy; R²≈0.8 holds for *diversified portfolios*, far lower for single funds. Good for exposure mapping; alpha t-tests underpowered as above | Shrink (exposures); Noise (single-fund alpha significance) |
| Credit factor models | Credit beta vs. selection alpha | T1 | Smoothed NAVs bias betas toward zero and understate vol; must unsmooth (GLM 2004) first, which itself consumes df and adds estimator variance | Shrink |
| Sharpe ratio / IR comparisons | Risk-adjusted return | T1 | SE(SR)≈√(1/T)·√(1+SR²/2) ≈ 0.5 annualized at T=48; use HAC/bootstrap CIs (Lo; Ledoit–Wolf), report intervals not points | Shrink |
| Active share | Deviation from benchmark | T3 (T2 approx.) | Point-in-time *measurement*, no time-series sampling error; robust as a descriptor. Its *return-prediction* claim is contested — do not treat as a skill signal | Robust (as measurement) |
| Information coefficient | Signal–outcome correlation | T3 | SE(IC per period)≈1/√breadth; with breadth ≥100 names and 48 periods, mean IC of 0.03–0.05 is detectable. Breadth <30 (concentrated L/S): not detectable | Robust (high-breadth); Noise (concentrated books) |
| Hit rate / slugging | Win frequency / win-loss size | T3 | Binomial: distinguishing 55% vs 50% at 80% power needs ~780 independent trades. High-turnover quant clears this in 1–2 yrs; a 30-name fundamental book never does in 5 | Shrink (gate on trade count) |
| Trade-level skill decomposition | Entry/exit/sizing/holding contributions | T3 | Power scales with # round-trips; overlapping positions and common factor moves cut effective N materially — cluster/bootstrap SEs mandatory | Shrink |
| Sizing-skill curves | Conviction-weighting value vs. equal-weight counterfactual | T3 | Curve estimation splits the trade sample into buckets; needs several hundred trades per curve. Low confidence: little published small-sample validation exists | Shrink |
| Alpha-decay curves | Post-trade return horizon profile | T3 | Event-study across trades (Di Mascio–Lines–Naik use 2001–2013 transaction panels); with ≥300–500 trades the shape is estimable; monthly 13F snapshots degrade it badly | Shrink |
| Performance persistence tests | Rank stability across periods | T1 | Splitting 48 obs into rank/eval windows on tens of managers compounds two noisy estimates; rank correlation CIs at n=30 span ±0.35 | Noise |
| Hierarchical/Bayesian shrinkage | Posterior alpha pooling the cross-section | T1 | *Designed* for this regime (Baks–Metrick–Wachter; Kosowski–Naik–Teo; Harvey–Liu random effects). Caveat: with only tens of funds the hyperparameters (cross-sectional alpha dispersion) are themselves noisy — use informative priors, report sensitivity | Robust (the method of choice) |
| FDR control (Barras–Scaillet–Wermers) | Fraction of lucky vs. skilled funds | T1 | Calibrated on 2,000+ funds; π₀ estimation is unstable at n = tens; individual-fund p-values are already weak inputs | Noise (at tens of managers) |
| Drawdown-distribution analysis | Tail/failure signature | T1 | Max DD over 36–60 obs is dominated by one episode; extreme-value inference impossible. Simulation-calibrated DD alarm thresholds given an assumed SR (van Hemert et al. 2020) are legitimate | Shrink (simulation-calibrated rules only) |
| Regime-conditional performance | Alpha by macro/vol regime | T1 | Splitting 48 obs leaves 10–20 per regime; SEs roughly double; any "crisis alpha" claim at this N is anecdote | Noise (inference); descriptive only |

| Portfolio-overlap crowding | Shared-position concentration | T2/T3 (or 13F) | Point-in-time measurement — no small-T problem. Predictive validity for tail risk documented (Brown–Howard–Lundblad 2022) on long US equity only | Robust (measurement) |
| Short-interest crowding | Short-side congestion | Market data | Uses market-wide data, not manager history; robust descriptively; low confidence as a stand-alone redemption trigger | Robust (descriptive) |
| Liquidity-adjusted concentration (days-to-cover style) | Exit-cost-weighted crowding | T2/T3 + volume | Measurement, not estimation; the volume denominator is itself regime-dependent — stress it | Robust (measurement) |

## 2. Best sources (annotated links)

- **Lo (2002), "The Statistics of Sharpe Ratios," FAJ** — [PDF](https://traders.studentorg.berkeley.edu/papers/The-Statistics-of-Sharpe-Ratios.pdf). The canonical SR standard-error and annualization-under-autocorrelation results; the quantitative backbone of every "Noise" verdict above.
- **Ledoit & Wolf (2008), "Robust performance hypothesis testing with the Sharpe ratio," JEF** — [PDF](http://www.ledoit.net/jef_2008pdf.pdf). Studentized bootstrap CIs for SR differences under fat tails/serial dependence; code available.
- **Getmansky, Lo & Makarov (2004), JFE** — [PDF](http://web.mit.edu/Alo/www/Papers/JFE2004Pub.pdf). Smoothing/illiquidity model; mandatory pre-processing before any credit or illiquid-strategy regression.
- **Fung & Hsieh (2004), "Hedge Fund Benchmarks: A Risk-Based Approach," FAJ** — [PDF](https://people.duke.edu/~dah7/HF-RF.pdf); factor data are maintained on Hsieh's Duke data library (same site). The macro/trend exposure model; note the 80% R² claim applies to diversified HF portfolios.
- **Pástor & Stambaugh (2002), "Mutual Fund Performance and Seemingly Unrelated Assets," JFE** — [repository page](https://repository.upenn.edu/fnce_papers/364/). Shows how non-benchmark assets sharpen alpha estimates — directly exploitable at small T.
- **Baks, Metrick & Wachter (2001), JF** — [PDF](https://finance.wharton.upenn.edu/~jwachter/research/BaksMetrickWachter2001jf.pdf). Bayesian priors over skill; the template for "how skeptical should the prior be."
- **Kosowski, Naik & Teo (2007), JFE** — [PDF](https://ink.library.smu.edu.sg/cgi/viewcontent.cgi?article=1007&context=bnp_research). Bayesian alphas beat OLS alphas for hedge-fund prediction — evidence shrinkage pays in exactly our asset class.
- **Harvey & Liu (2018), "Detecting Repeatable Performance," RFS** — [PDF](https://people.duke.edu/~charvey/Research/Published_Papers/P132_Detecting_repeatable_performance.pdf). Random-effects noise reduction; the modern reference implementation for hierarchical alpha shrinkage.
- **Barras, Scaillet & Wermers (2010), JF** — [PDF](https://terpconnect.umd.edu/~wermers/FDR_published.pdf). FDR machinery — read to understand why it *doesn't* transfer to n = tens.
- **Cremers & Petajisto (2009), RFS** — [SSRN](https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=891719). Active share definition and original evidence (subsequent replication debate should temper the predictive claim).
- **Di Mascio, Lines & Naik, "Alpha Decay"** — [PDF](https://jhfinance.web.unc.edu/wp-content/uploads/sites/12369/2016/02/Alpha-Decay.pdf) / [SSRN](https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=2580551). Transaction-level term structure of institutional alpha; the model for trade-level analytics.
- **Bollen & Whaley (2009), "Hedge Fund Risk Dynamics," JF** — [SSRN](https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=937972). Changepoint regressions for shifting exposures; calibrates how much sample style-drift detection really needs.
- **van Hemert, Ganz, Harvey, Rattray et al. (2020), "Drawdowns," JPM** — [PDF](https://people.duke.edu/~charvey/Research/Published_Papers/P147_Drawdowns.pdf). Simulation-based drawdown alarm thresholds — the right way to use DD at small T.
- **Brown, Howard & Lundblad (2022), "Crowded Trades and Tail Risk," RFS** — [PDF](https://uncipc.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/02/CTTR.pdf). Security-level crowdedness from HF holdings predicts drawdowns in industry distress.
- **Grinold & Kahn, *Active Portfolio Management*** (McGraw-Hill; book, no free official PDF). The fundamental law IR ≈ IC·√BR underpins all breadth-based power reasoning here.

## 3. White space

- **Small-sample power tables for holdings-based metrics barely exist in public literature.** The binomial/breadth arithmetic in Section 1 is straightforward, but I found no published study that systematically maps "trades per year × years → detectable skill" for hit rate, slugging, or sizing curves. Building and publishing (internally) those power curves is cheap and differentiating. (Flag: the sizing-skill row is the lowest-confidence verdict in the table for this reason.)
- **No standard framework fuses transparency tiers.** Hierarchical Bayes on T1 returns and event studies on T3 trades exist separately; a single posterior over manager skill that conditions on whatever tier is available — with holdings evidence tightening the returns-only prior — appears to be unpublished territory.
- **Unsmoothing + inference pipelines are rarely combined.** GLM unsmoothing and Lo/Ledoit–Wolf SR inference are almost never composed end-to-end in practice; credit-manager Sharpe CIs that propagate unsmoothing uncertainty are white space.
- **Crowding validation is long-only US-equity-centric.** Brown–Howard–Lundblad is 13F longs; short-side, credit, and macro crowding measures lack comparable public validation. Low confidence attaches to any cross-strategy crowding score until this gap closes.
- **FDR-style luck control at n = tens is unsolved**; empirical-Bayes with an informative π₀ prior borrowed from published fund panels is a plausible but untested substitute.

## 4. Implications for idea cards

- **Hierarchical Bayesian alpha engine (T1, all managers).** Random-effects shrinkage of factor alphas across the roster, per Harvey–Liu/Kosowski et al., replacing per-manager t-tests. Improves **select** (rank on posterior mean, not noisy point alpha) and **size** (posterior uncertainty feeds position sizing directly).
- **Sharpe/IR "interval-only" reporting standard.** Every performance stat ships with Lo/Ledoit–Wolf CIs computed on GLM-unsmoothed returns; dashboards suppress point rankings that intervals cannot separate. Improves **monitor** and prevents false-conviction **redeem** decisions triggered by noise.
- **Trade-count-gated skill decomposition (T3 managers).** Hit rate, slugging, IC, and alpha-decay analytics run only when the book clears pre-computed power thresholds (e.g., ~800 independent trades for hit-rate discrimination); below threshold the card renders "insufficient N," not a number. Improves **engage** — conversations with managers cite decomposition only where it is statistically real — and improves **select** among high-turnover quant managers where it genuinely discriminates.
- **Simulation-calibrated drawdown alarms.** Per-manager DD thresholds derived from the van Hemert et al. framework given the manager's assumed Sharpe and correlation profile, replacing ad-hoc "down 15% = review" rules. Improves **redeem** (alarm fires when DD is inconsistent with the maintained skill hypothesis) and **monitor**.
- **Exposure-drift monitor from T2 risk reports, not returns.** Direct measurement of factor-exposure drift from manager risk summaries, with returns-based rolling betas demoted to a fallback flagged as low-power. Improves **monitor** and **engage** (specific, measured drift to discuss) and avoids noise-theater style-drift accusations.
- **Cross-manager overlap and liquidity-adjusted crowding map (T2/T3 + 13F).** Pairwise position overlap and days-to-cover-weighted concentration across the roster, stress-tested to depressed volumes. Improves **size** (cap aggregate exposure to crowded themes across managers) and **redeem** (crowded, illiquid books exit first in stress).
- **Do-not-build list.** Standalone persistence rankings, FDR luck-screens at roster scale, regime-split alpha tables, and conditional-beta alphas are gated out at 36–60 observations; each may be revisited at platform level if the evaluable universe reaches hundreds of funds. Protects **select** from methods whose findings would be indistinguishable from luck.
