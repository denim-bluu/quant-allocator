# Multi-Manager "PM Engagement" Analytics: A Landscape Brief for a Tiered-Transparency Allocator

## 1. State of practice

Multi-manager platforms have institutionalized a function that sits between central risk and the trading desk: a quantitative, PM-facing analytics team whose job is to make each portfolio manager more scalable and profitable. Balyasny calls it **PM Engagement**; Citadel houses the analog in its **Portfolio Construction and Risk Group** (reports to the CEO, staffed with strategy-specific specialists rather than generalists); Point72 blends it into **Market Intelligence** plus the **LaunchPoint** emerging-PM coaching program; Millennium enforces more of it algorithmically. The framing across all four is consistent and worth stealing: risk analytics is sold to PMs as *"a backbone to an alpha-generation process,"* not as a compliance leash (a point Dmitry Balyasny makes directly about the risk head he hired from Citadel after 2018 losses). The Balyasny PM Engagement job descriptions are the single most concrete public artifact — they explicitly list P&L driver analysis, factor/factor-model research, portfolio-construction/hedging coaching, and "best practices" capture as the work product.

Below is a reconstructed catalog. Data tiers: **R** = monthly returns only; **E** = exposure/risk-report summaries (factor exposures, gross/net, sector buckets); **P** = position-level transparency (holdings + ideally trade timestamps). Tier shown is the *minimum* for a credible version; most analytics get sharper one tier up.

| # | Analytic | What it measures | Min. tier |
|---|----------|------------------|-----------|
| 1 | **Factor vs. idiosyncratic return attribution** | Splits P&L into intentional stock picks vs. "accidental" factor bets (the canonical "8% came from a momentum overweight, 3% from picks, 1% noise" decomposition via `r = xᵀf + ε`) | **E** (crude via **R** returns-regression) |
| 2 | **Factor-hygiene review** | Style tilts kept inside bands (e.g. ±0.5σ), beta/sector neutrality, unintended vs. intended exposures over time | **E** |
| 3 | **Gross/net & hedging discipline** | Gross leverage (typ. 4–8x on allocated capital), net exposure bands (~ −20%/+20%), hedge-basket usage when limits breach | **E** |
| 4 | **Sizing / conviction curves** | Do bigger positions earn more per unit gross? Maps conviction/size to realized alpha; flags "optimal vs. actual" sizing gap (Alpha Theory reports a ~4.3%/yr gap; Novus studies position-sizing alpha) | **P** |
| 5 | **Alpha-decay curves** | Cumulative alpha vs. holding period after entry — identifies whether edge is front-loaded (held too long) or slow to mature (sized too early) | **P** (needs trade dates) |
| 6 | **Hit rate (batting average) & slugging ratio** | Win % of positions and avg-winner/avg-loser; skill can come from either. ~50–60% batting is typical L/S; slugging near/above ~1.3–1.65x signals real edge | **P** |
| 7 | **Entry/exit-timing & scaling attribution** | Decision-attribution: value added/destroyed by buy timing, scaling in/out, and exit timing separately (Essentia's "Behavioral Alpha" taxonomy is the reference implementation) | **P** (transaction-level) |
| 8 | **PM report cards / scorecards** | Rolling Sharpe (30/90/ITD), drawdown-from-peak, return-on-gross, factor share of P&L, turnover, holding period — the periodic "how is this book really doing" panel | **R** (richer at **E**) |
| 9 | **Crowding dashboards** | Cross-book overlap, hedge-fund-ownership (HFOWN) and passive-ownership factors, days-to-cover, "squeeze risk" scores; at platform level, aggregate factor crowding across all pods | **P** (public proxy via 13F/short-interest) |
| 10 | **Short-book quality** | Is short alpha real and separable from the long book? Borrow cost/utilization, float-awareness, squeeze exposure, whether shorts are alpha or just beta hedges | **P** |
| 11 | **Liquidity / capacity analysis** | Days-to-liquidate, market-impact of unwinding, position size vs. ADV — feeds scalability judgments | **P** (approx. at **E**) |
| 12 | **Macro/stress tail exposures** | Sensitivity to rate shocks, credit-spread widening, vol spikes; scenario/stress P&L | **E** (precise at **P**) |
| 13 | **VaR / vol-target / drawdown governance** | Vol targeting, VaR limits, automatic de-risking triggers (Millennium's widely-reported ~5% → cut, ~7.5% → wind-down thresholds) | **R** for vol/DD; **E** for VaR |
| 14 | **Center-book / replication value** | Platform replicates a subset of PM trades using knowledge of each PM's strengths/behavioral tics ("God's-eye view") | **P**, firm-internal (see §3) |

**Engagement model.** The recurring pattern: *daily* factor-exposure reports every morning; an optimizer that proposes hedge baskets pre-trade when a proposed position breaches limits; and periodic (monthly/quarterly) face-to-face reviews where a quant sits with the PM to walk the report card and attribution. Job specs demand "outstanding written and oral communication" and the ability to "coach and educate fundamental PMs" — the analytics only matter if the PM acts on them. Essentia's finding that managers who engage with behavioral "nudges" beat non-engagers by ~160 bps/yr is the clearest public evidence that *delivery* (cadence + framing + a credible human partner, often an ex-PM) is as important as the math. *Confidence: high on the shape of the model, medium on specifics, since most detail comes from job posts and secondary Substack write-ups rather than firm disclosures.*

## 3. White space

- **The tier-degradation map is unpublished.** Nobody has a public, honest table of "here is the platform-grade analytic, here is the best returns-only or exposure-only approximation, and here is the error you eat." That map is exactly what a tiered allocator needs and is a defensible internal asset.
- **Returns-only proxies for sizing/decay.** Sizing curves, alpha decay, and batting/slugging are treated as strictly position-level. There is little public work on *inferring* sizing discipline or alpha-decay signatures from monthly-return time series (e.g., autocorrelation, return-on-vol stability, style-drift regressions). High-value, low-confidence-that-it-works — worth an explicit research card.
- **Crowding without holdings.** Platforms compute crowding from actual books; allocators mostly can't. The gap: how far do 13F, short-interest, and factor-ownership proxies get you toward a manager-level crowding/squeeze score, and how stale is it?
- **Center-book replication does not transplant.** An allocator can't and shouldn't replicate external-manager trades. But the *underlying idea* — using known behavioral tics to predict where a manager leaks alpha — can be reframed as a monitoring/redemption signal from whatever tier you have.
- **Engagement-as-product for external managers.** Platforms coach captive PMs; allocators are structurally at arm's length. There's white space in a "manager engagement" offering (share select analytics back to managers) that improves relationship and data access without crossing into control.

## 4. Implications for idea cards

- **PM Report Card (returns-only), improves MONITOR & SELECT.** Rolling Sharpe/drawdown/return-on-gross plus a returns-based factor regression, runnable for *every* manager from the monthly tier. It is the universal baseline that flags which managers deserve a deeper exposure/position request. Frame it as the always-on layer.
- **Sizing-curve & alpha-decay diagnostics (position-level), improves SIZE & REDEEM.** For the few position-transparent managers, quantify whether their edge is in picking vs. sizing vs. timing and whether they hold winners too long. A manager whose alpha is real but whose sizing is inverted is a *coach-or-resize* case, not a redeem — this directly informs how much capital to give and whether decay is worsening (a redemption trigger).
- **Factor-hygiene & gross/net discipline panel (exposure summaries), improves MONITOR & ENGAGE.** From exposure reports alone, track whether a manager's realized returns are increasingly "accidental factor" vs. intended alpha, and whether they respect their own stated net/gross bands. This is the single most transplantable platform analytic to the middle tier and the natural centerpiece of a quarterly engagement conversation.
- **Cross-manager crowding & correlation dashboard (position-level where available, 13F/short-interest proxy elsewhere), improves SELECT & MONITOR.** Aggregate overlap and factor-crowding across the external book is the allocator's version of the platform's firm-wide surveillance — it catches the "everyone is long the same momentum unwind" risk the manager-level reports miss. Flag the proxy version as lower-confidence.
- **Short-book quality score (position-level), improves SIZE & REDEEM.** Separate short alpha from beta-hedge shorts, layer borrow/utilization and squeeze risk. A manager whose shorts are pure hedges is really a beta-timing bet and should be sized/underwritten as such.
- **Behavioral-attribution engagement pack (position/transaction-level), improves ENGAGE & MONITOR.** Package entry/exit-timing and scaling attribution (Essentia-style) as material shared *back* to transparent managers. The public 160 bps engagement result argues this both improves the manager and deepens the data relationship — turning transparency into a two-way, renewable asset.

*Overall confidence: medium-high on the analytics catalog and tiering logic; medium on engagement cadence specifics; low on any internal-tool detail (none is publicly documented — treated as reconstruction, not fact).*
